# FIFA World Cup 2026 — Stadiums & Host Cities

The 2026 tournament is the first hosted across three countries (USA, Mexico,
Canada) with 16 stadiums and 48 teams. It runs 11 June – 19 July 2026.

## United States (11 stadiums)
- Atlanta — Mercedes-Benz Stadium (FIFA: "Atlanta Stadium"), capacity 68,239.
  Accessible entrances on all levels; MARTA rail connects to the gate.
- Boston — Gillette Stadium (FIFA: "Boston Stadium"), capacity 64,146.
  Accessible entrance at the North Putnam lot; shuttle from Foxborough station.
- Dallas (Arlington) — AT&T Stadium (FIFA: "Dallas Stadium"), capacity 70,649.
  Hosts the most matches (9). Accessible entry at Entry A.
- Houston — NRG Stadium (FIFA: "Houston Stadium"), capacity 68,777.
  Connected to the Metro Rail Red Line.
- Kansas City — Arrowhead Stadium (FIFA: "Kansas City Stadium"), capacity 69,045.
  Accessible seating in Sections 106/107.
- Los Angeles — SoFi Stadium (FIFA: "Los Angeles Stadium"), capacity 70,492.
  Accessible entry via the Inglewood Transit connector.
- Miami — Hard Rock Stadium (FIFA: "Miami Stadium"), capacity 64,478.
  Accessible gates at the North and South lower bowls.
- New York/New Jersey (East Rutherford) — MetLife Stadium
  (FIFA: "New York New Jersey Stadium"), capacity 80,663. Hosts the FINAL
  on 19 July 2026. Accessible entry at the MetLife West.
- Philadelphia — Lincoln Financial Field (FIFA: "Philadelphia Stadium"),
  capacity 68,324.
- San Francisco Bay Area (Santa Clara) — Levi's Stadium
  (FIFA: "Bay Area Stadium"), capacity 68,827.
- Seattle — Lumen Field (FIFA: "Seattle Stadium"), capacity 66,925.
  Light rail (Link) connects directly to the stadium.

## Mexico (3 stadiums)
- Guadalajara (Zapopan) — Estadio Akron (FIFA: "Estadio Guadalajara"),
  capacity 45,664.
- Mexico City — Estadio Azteca (FIFA: "Estadio Azteca"), capacity 80,824.
  Hosts the opening match. First stadium to host three World Cups (1970, 1986, 2026).
- Monterrey (Guadalupe) — Estadio BBVA, capacity 51,243.

## Canada (2 stadiums)
- Toronto — BMO Field, capacity 43,036.
- Vancouver — BC Place, capacity 52,497.
